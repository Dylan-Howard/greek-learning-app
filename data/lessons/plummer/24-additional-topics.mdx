## Conditional Sentences — Summary

Greek has four conditional types, distinguished by form:

| Class | Protasis (if-clause) | Apodosis | Meaning |
|-------|---------------------|----------|---------|
| **1st** | εἰ + indicative | any | "If (assumed true)..." |
| **2nd** | εἰ + past indicative | past ind. + ἄν | "If (but it's not)..." |
| **3rd** | ἐάν + subjunctive | any | "If (probable future)..." |
| **4th** | εἰ + optative | optative + ἄν | "If (remote possibility)..." |

## Questions in Greek

**Direct questions** may be introduced by:
- Interrogative pronoun **τίς, τί** (*who? what?*)
- Interrogative adverbs: ποῦ (*where?*), πότε (*when?*), πῶς (*how?*), διατί (*why?*)
- The particle **ἆρα** (expects uncertain answer)
- The particle **οὐ** at the start (expects "yes")
- The particle **μή** at the start (expects "no")

## The Optative Mood

The **optative** expresses a wish or remote possibility. Rare in the NT (67 occurrences), but important:

- **μὴ γένοιτο** — "May it never be! / God forbid!" (Paul's rhetorical protest, 15× in Romans/Galatians)
- εἴη (*may he/it be*) — optative of εἰμί

## Indirect Discourse — Summary

Three structures for reporting what someone said/thought:

| Structure | Signal | Example |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| **ὅτι + indicative** | λέγω ὅτι | "he says **that** it is true" |
| **Infinitive** | verbs of perception | "he believes him **to be** wise" |
| **Participle** | verbs of knowing/seeing | "I saw him **leaving**" |
