## Greek Adjectives

Adjectives **agree** with the noun they modify in **gender, number, and case**. The most common type (2-1-2) uses second declension endings for masculine and neuter, first declension for feminine.

### ἀγαθός, -ή, -όν (*good*) — Nominative Singular

| Masc. | Fem. | Neut. |
|-------|------|-------|
| ἀγαθός | ἀγαθή | ἀγαθόν |

For all other cases, apply the same endings as nouns.

## Attributive vs. Predicate Position

**Attributive** (adjective inside the article-noun unit) — directly modifies:
- ὁ **ἀγαθὸς** ἄνθρωπος — *the good man*
- ὁ ἄνθρωπος ὁ **ἀγαθός** — *the good man*

**Predicate** (adjective outside the unit) — makes a statement:
- **ἀγαθὸς** ὁ ἄνθρωπος — *The man is good.*
- ὁ ἄνθρωπος **ἀγαθός** — *The man is good.*

**Substantive** (adjective + article, no noun) — functions as a noun:
- οἱ **ἀγαθοί** — *the good ones / the good men*
- τὸ **ἀγαθόν** — *the good thing / goodness*
