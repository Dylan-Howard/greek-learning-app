## First Declension — Feminine Nouns

First declension nouns are mostly **feminine** and end in **-η** or **-α** in the nominative singular. The stem ends in a vowel, which merges with case endings.

### -η Stem: φωνή (*voice, sound*)

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nom. | φων**ή** | φων**αί** |
| Gen. | φων**ῆς** | φων**ῶν** |
| Dat. | φων**ῇ** | φων**αῖς** |
| Acc. | φων**ήν** | φων**άς** |
| Voc. | φων**ή** | φων**αί** |

### -α (long) Stem: ἀρχή (*beginning, ruler*)

Same pattern as φωνή. When the stem vowel is long ā, it replaces η throughout.

### -α Stem after ε, ι, ρ: δόξα (*glory*)

After ε, ι, or ρ, the α is retained (not switched to η) in singular:

| Case | Singular |
|------|----------|
| Nom. | δόξ**α** |
| Gen. | δόξ**ης** |
| Dat. | δόξ**ῃ** |
| Acc. | δόξ**αν** |

The plural of all first declension nouns is the **same**: -αί, -ῶν, -αῖς, -άς.
