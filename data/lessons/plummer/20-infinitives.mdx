## The Infinitive

The infinitive is a **verbal noun** — it names an action but has no person or number. It has tense (aspect) and voice.

## Infinitive Forms

| Tense/Voice | Ending | Example |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| Pres. active | -ειν | λύ**ειν** (*to be loosing*) |
| Pres. mid/pass | -εσθαι | λύ**εσθαι** |
| Aor. active (1st) | -σαι | λύ**σαι** (*to loose*) |
| Aor. passive | -θῆναι | λυ**θῆναι** |
| Perf. active | -κέναι | λελυ**κέναι** (*to have loosed*) |

> No augment on any infinitive. Augment = indicative only.

## Uses of the Infinitive

**1. Complementary** (completes an incomplete verb):
- δύναται **λύειν** — "he is able **to loose**"
- θέλω **πιστεύειν** — "I want **to believe**"

**2. Purpose** (answers "why?"):
- ἦλθεν **σῴσαι** τὸν κόσμον — "He came **to save** the world."

**3. Articular** (neuter article + infinitive):
- τοῦ + inf. → purpose/result: "in order to"
- ἐν τῷ + inf. → "while ___-ing"
- μετὰ τό + inf. → "after ___-ing"
- πρὸ τοῦ + inf. → "before ___-ing"

**4. Indirect discourse** (after verbs of saying/thinking):
- λέγει αὐτὸν **εἶναι** προφήτην — "She says that he **is** a prophet."
