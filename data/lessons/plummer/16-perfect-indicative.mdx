## The Perfect Indicative

The **perfect** expresses a **past completed action with ongoing present results**. It is the tense of lasting consequence.

- γέγραπται — *it is written* (and stands written) — used when citing Scripture
- τετέλεσται — *it is finished* (John 19:30) — the work is done and the result stands forever

## Formation — Reduplication

The perfect is marked by **reduplication**: the first consonant of the verb is repeated with **ε**, forming a prefix.

- λύω → **λε**λυ- → λέλυκα
- γράφω → **γε**γραφ- → γέγραφα → (κ aorist) γέγραφα

For verbs beginning with a vowel, the vowel **lengthens** (like augment):
- ἀγαπάω → **ἠγ**άπηκα

## Perfect Active — λέλυκα (*I have loosed*)

Tense formative: **κ** (active only)

| Person | Sg. | Pl. |
|--------|-----|-----|
| 1st | λέλυ**κα** | λελύ**καμεν** |
| 2nd | λέλυ**κας** | λελύ**κατε** |
| 3rd | λέλυ**κε(ν)** | λελύ**κασι(ν)** |

## Perfect Middle/Passive — λέλυμαι (*I have been loosed*)

No κ formative; reduplication + primary middle/passive endings directly:

| Person | Sg. | Pl. |
|--------|-----|-----|
| 1st | λέλυ**μαι** | λελύ**μεθα** |
| 2nd | λέλυ**σαι** | λέλυ**σθε** |
| 3rd | λέλυ**ται** | λέλυ**νται** |
