## The Participle — Verbal Adjective

A **participle** combines verbal and adjectival properties. It has tense (aspect), voice, and can take an object — but also has gender, number, and case to agree with a noun.

## Present Active Participle

Suffix: **-ων, -ουσα, -ον** (masc./neut. = 3rd declension; fem. = 1st declension)

**λύω → λύων, λύουσα, λῦον** (*loosing, while loosing*)

| | Masc. | Fem. | Neut. |
|-|-------|------|-------|
| Nom. Sg. | λύ**ων** | λύ**ουσα** | λῦ**ον** |
| Gen. Sg. | λύ**οντος** | λυ**ούσης** | λύ**οντος** |

## Three Uses of the Participle

| Use | Article? | Function |
|-----|----------|---------|
| **Adverbial** | No | Modifies main verb (temporal, causal, means) |
| **Adjectival** | Yes | Modifies a noun (attributive position) |
| **Substantive** | Yes, no noun | Acts as a noun |

**Adverbial** (simultaneous action): λύ**ων** εἶπεν — *While loosing, he said...*
**Adjectival**: ὁ λύ**ων** ἄνθρωπος — *the loosing man*
**Substantive**: ὁ λύ**ων** — *the one who is loosing*

## Present Middle/Passive Participle

Suffix: **-όμενος, -ομένη, -όμενον** (2-1-2 adjective pattern)

## Relative Time

- **Present participle** = action **simultaneous** with the main verb
- **Aorist participle** = action **prior** to the main verb
