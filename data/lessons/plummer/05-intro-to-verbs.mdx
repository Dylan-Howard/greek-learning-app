## The Greek Verb System — Overview

Greek verbs encode six pieces of information:

| Component | Options |
|-----------|---------|
| **Person** | 1st, 2nd, 3rd |
| **Number** | Singular, Plural |
| **Tense** | Present, Imperfect, Future, Aorist, Perfect, Pluperfect |
| **Voice** | Active, Middle, Passive |
| **Mood** | Indicative, Subjunctive, Imperative, Optative |
| **Lexical form** | The verb's dictionary entry |

To parse a verb is to identify all six. Example: λύομεν = present / active / indicative / 1st / plural / λύω (*I loose*) — "we are loosing."

## Verbal Aspect — The Core Concept

Greek tense is fundamentally about **aspect** — the kind of action — not just time:

- **Present tense** → **Continuous** aspect: the action is in progress
- **Aorist tense** → **Undefined/Punctiliar** aspect: a simple action, viewed as a whole
- **Perfect tense** → **Completed** aspect: a past action with a present ongoing result

Time is indicated by the indicative mood + tense combination. In other moods (subjunctive, imperative), only aspect matters, not time.

## Present Active Indicative of λύω

The formula: **tense stem** + **connecting vowel** (o/e) + **personal ending**

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | λύ**ω** | λύ**ομεν** |
| 2nd | λύ**εις** | λύ**ετε** |
| 3rd | λύ**ει** | λύ**ουσι(ν)** |
