## Aorist Adverbial Participles

The **aorist participle** expresses action **prior to** the main verb. It is the most common participial construction in NT narrative.

> **Key:** Always remove the augment before forming a participle.

## Aorist Active Participle

Suffix: **-σας, -σασα, -σαν** (3rd declension masc./neut.; 1st declension fem.)

**λύω → λύσας, λύσασα, λῦσαν** (*having loosed, after loosing*)

| | Masc. | Fem. | Neut. |
|-|-------|------|-------|
| Nom. Sg. | λύ**σας** | λύ**σασα** | λῦ**σαν** |
| Gen. Sg. | λύ**σαντος** | λυ**σάσης** | λύ**σαντος** |

## Second Aorist Active Participle

Uses the second aorist stem + **-ών, -οῦσα, -όν** (same suffixes as present active but with aorist stem — accent distinguishes them):

- βάλλω → βαλ**ών**, βαλ**οῦσα**, βαλ**όν** (*having thrown*)

## Aorist Passive Participle

Suffix: **-θείς, -θεῖσα, -θέν**

- λύω → λυ**θείς**, λυ**θεῖσα**, λυ**θέν** (*having been loosed*)

## The Genitive Absolute

When the participle and its noun/pronoun stand in the **genitive**, grammatically independent from the main clause, it is a **genitive absolute** expressing attendant circumstances.

- **αὐτοῦ λέγοντος** — "While he was speaking..." (lit. "him speaking")
- **ταῦτα εἰπόντος αὐτοῦ** — "After he had said these things..."
