## The Imperative Mood

The **imperative** gives commands. Greek imperatives come in 2nd and 3rd person, in present (continuous) and aorist (undefined) aspect.

## Aspect in Commands

- **Present imperative** → command for ongoing/repeated action
  - "Keep praying" · "Always rejoice" · "Don't stop loving"
- **Aorist imperative** → command for a specific act
  - "Pray!" (now, do it) · "Believe!" · "Go!"

## Present Active Imperative — λύω

| Person | Sg. | Pl. |
|--------|-----|-----|
| 2nd | λῦ**ε** | λύ**ετε** |
| 3rd | λυ**έτω** | λυ**έτωσαν** |

## Aorist Active Imperative — λύω

| Person | Sg. | Pl. |
|--------|-----|-----|
| 2nd | λῦ**σον** | λύ**σατε** |
| 3rd | λυ**σάτω** | λυ**σάτωσαν** |

## Prohibitions

- **μή + present imperative** → Stop doing (cease an ongoing action)
  - μὴ κλαίετε — "Stop weeping."
- **μή + aorist subjunctive** → Don't begin (prevent a not-yet-started action)
  - μὴ φοβηθῇς — "Do not be afraid."

## Common Irregular Imperatives

| Verb | 2nd Sg. Aor. Act. Imp. | Meaning |
|------|-----------------------|---------|
| δίδωμι | δός | Give! |
| λέγω | εἰπέ | Say! |
| ἔρχομαι | ἐλθέ | Come! |
| τίθημι | θές | Put! |
| ἵστημι | στῆθι | Stand! |
| ὁράω | ἰδέ / ἰδού | Look! Behold! |
