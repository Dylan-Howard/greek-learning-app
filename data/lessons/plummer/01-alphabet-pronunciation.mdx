## Why Study New Testament Greek?

The New Testament was written in **Koine Greek** — not a translation, but the original language of the authors. Every English Bible you read is an interpretation. Greek study lets you engage the text one layer closer to what Paul, Luke, John, and the other writers actually wrote.

Koine (κοινή, *common*) Greek was the everyday language of the first-century Mediterranean world, spread by Alexander the Great's conquests. It was used from Egypt to Persia as the shared language of culture, commerce, and administration.

## The Greek Alphabet — 24 Letters

| # | Upper | Lower | Name | Sound |
|---|-------|-------|------|-------|
| 1 | Α | α | Alpha | *a* as in f**a**ther |
| 2 | Β | β | Beta | *b* |
| 3 | Γ | γ | Gamma | *g* (ng before γ,κ,χ,ξ) |
| 4 | Δ | δ | Delta | *d* |
| 5 | Ε | ε | Epsilon | short *e* |
| 6 | Ζ | ζ | Zeta | *dz* / *z* |
| 7 | Η | η | Eta | long *a* (always long) |
| 8 | Θ | θ | Theta | *th* (as in **th**eory) |
| 9 | Ι | ι | Iota | short or long *i* |
| 10 | Κ | κ | Kappa | *k* |
| 11 | Λ | λ | Lambda | *l* |
| 12 | Μ | μ | Mu | *m* |
| 13 | Ν | ν | Nu | *n* |
| 14 | Ξ | ξ | Xi | *x* (as in bo**x**) |
| 15 | Ο | ο | Omicron | short *o* (always short) |
| 16 | Π | π | Pi | *p* |
| 17 | Ρ | ρ | Rho | *r* |
| 18 | Σ | σ/ς | Sigma | *s* (ς at word end) |
| 19 | Τ | τ | Tau | *t* |
| 20 | Υ | υ | Upsilon | *u* (German ü) |
| 21 | Φ | φ | Phi | *ph* (as in **ph**one) |
| 22 | Χ | χ | Chi | breathy *ch* (as in lo**ch**) |
| 23 | Ψ | ψ | Psi | *ps* (as in li**ps**) |
| 24 | Ω | ω | Omega | long *o* (always long) |

## Diphthongs

| Diphthong | Sound | Example |
|-----------|-------|---------|
| αι | *ai* (aisle) | καί |
| αυ | *au* (out) | αὐτός |
| ει | *ei* (eight) | εἰς |
| ευ | *eu* (feud) | εὐθύς |
| οι | *oi* (oil) | οἶκος |
| ου | *oo* (moon) | οὐ |

## Breathing Marks

Every word beginning with a vowel has a breathing mark over the first vowel:
- **Smooth** (᾿) — no extra sound: ἀπό (*from*)
- **Rough** (῾) — adds *h* sound: ὁ (*the*, pronounced *ho*)
