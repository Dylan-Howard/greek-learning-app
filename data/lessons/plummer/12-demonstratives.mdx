## Demonstrative Pronouns

### οὗτος (*this, these*) — proximal

The key rule: the demonstrative stands in **predicate position** — the noun still keeps its article, but no article precedes the demonstrative.

- **οὗτος** ὁ ἄνθρωπος — *this man* ✓
- ~~ὁ οὗτος ἄνθρωπος~~ — WRONG

| Case | Masc. | Fem. | Neut. |
|------|-------|------|-------|
| Nom. Sg. | οὗτος | αὕτη | τοῦτο |
| Gen. Sg. | τούτου | ταύτης | τούτου |
| Dat. Sg. | τούτῳ | ταύτῃ | τούτῳ |
| Acc. Sg. | τοῦτον | ταύτην | τοῦτο |

### ἐκεῖνος (*that, those*) — distal

Declines like a regular 2-1-2 adjective (ἐκεῖνος, ἐκείνη, ἐκεῖνο).

## Reflexive Pronouns

Reflexive pronouns refer back to the subject. They only occur in oblique cases (gen., dat., acc.) — no nominative since they can't be the subject.

| Person | Masc. Acc. | Fem. Acc. |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| 1st sg. | ἐμαυτόν (*myself*) | ἐμαυτήν |
| 2nd sg. | σεαυτόν (*yourself*) | σεαυτήν |
| 3rd sg. | ἑαυτόν (*himself*) | ἑαυτήν | ἑαυτό |

## Reciprocal Pronoun

**ἀλλήλων** (*one another, each other*) — only plural, only oblique cases.
- ἀγαπᾶτε **ἀλλήλους** — "Love **one another**." (John 13:34)
