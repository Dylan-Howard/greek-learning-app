## The Aorist Indicative — Overview

The **aorist** expresses **undefined (punctiliar) aspect** — a simple, complete action viewed as a whole. In the indicative, it expresses **past time**. The aorist is the most common past tense in NT narrative.

There are two formation types:

| Type | Formation | Signal |
|------|-----------|--------|
| First aorist | augment + stem + **σα** + endings | σα formative |
| Second aorist | augment + **different stem** + endings | Different stem |

## First Aorist Active — ἔλυσα (*I loosed*)

**Formula:** augment + stem + **σα** + secondary active endings

| Person | Sg. | Pl. |
|--------|-----|-----|
| 1st | ἔλυ**σα** | ἐλύ**σαμεν** |
| 2nd | ἔλυ**σας** | ἐλύ**σατε** |
| 3rd | ἔλυ**σε(ν)** | ἔλυ**σαν** |

## Second Aorist Active — ἔβαλον (*I threw*)

Uses the secondary active endings but on a **different stem** (no σα):

| Person | Sg. | Pl. |
|--------|-----|-----|
| 1st | ἔβαλ**ον** | ἐβάλ**ομεν** |
| 2nd | ἔβαλ**ες** | ἐβάλ**ετε** |
| 3rd | ἔβαλ**ε(ν)** | ἔβαλ**ον** |

## Key Second Aorist Forms to Memorize

| Present | Aorist | Meaning |
|---------|--------|---------|
| ἔρχομαι | ἦλθον | I came |
| λέγω | εἶπον | I said |
| ὁράω | εἶδον | I saw |
| λαμβάνω | ἔλαβον | I took |
| εὑρίσκω | εὗρον | I found |
| ἐσθίω | ἔφαγον | I ate |
