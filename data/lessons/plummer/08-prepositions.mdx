## Greek Prepositions

Greek prepositions govern a specific case, and **meaning can shift by case**. The key prepositions:

| Preposition | + Genitive | + Dative | + Accusative | Freq. |
|-------------|-----------|----------|--------------|-------|
| ἀπό | from | — | — | 646 |
| διά | through | — | because of | 667 |
| εἰς | — | — | into, to | 1767 |
| ἐκ/ἐξ | out of | — | — | 914 |
| ἐν | — | in, with | — | 2752 |
| ἐπί | on, over | on, at | on, against | 890 |
| κατά | against | — | according to | 473 |
| μετά | with | — | after | 469 |
| παρά | from | beside | alongside | 194 |
| περί | about | — | around | 333 |
| πρός | — | — | to, toward | 700 |
| σύν | — | with | — | 128 |
| ὑπέρ | for | — | above | 150 |
| ὑπό | by | — | under | 220 |

> **ἐν always takes the dative.** No exceptions. Memorize this.
> **εἰς always takes the accusative.**

## εἰμί — Present Indicative

The verb "to be" (2,462 occurrences in NT):

| Person | Sg. | Pl. |
|--------|-----|-----|

| 2nd | εἶ | ἐστέ |
| 3rd | ἐστίν | εἰσίν |
