## The Future Indicative

The future tense expresses **undefined aspect in future time**. It is formed by adding the tense formative **σ** between the verbal root and the primary endings.

**Formula:** verbal root + **σ** + connecting vowel + primary active/middle endings

### Future Active — λύσω (*I will loose*)

| Person | Sg. | Pl. |
|--------|-----|-----|
| 1st | λύ**σω** | λύ**σομεν** |
| 2nd | λύ**σεις** | λύ**σετε** |
| 3rd | λύ**σει** | λύ**σουσι(ν)** |

### Consonant Changes Before σ

| Stem ending | + σ → |
|-------------|-------|
| Labials (π, β, φ) | ψ |
| Velars (κ, γ, χ) | ξ |
| Dentals (τ, δ, θ) | σ (dental drops) |

### Liquid Futures

Verbs with stems ending in λ, μ, ν, ρ use **ε** (not σ) as tense formative, then contract. They look like ε-contract verbs but have future meaning.

- κρίνω (*I judge*) → future: κρινῶ, κρινεῖς, κρινεῖ...

### Future of εἰμί

| Person | Sg. | Pl. |
|--------|-----|-----|
| 1st | ἔσομαι | ἐσόμεθα |
| 2nd | ἔσῃ | ἔσεσθε |
| 3rd | ἔσται | ἔσονται |
