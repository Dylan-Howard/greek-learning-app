## Personal Pronouns

### First Person: ἐγώ / ἡμεῖς

| Case | Sg. | Pl. |
|------|-----|-----|
| Nom. | ἐγώ | ἡμεῖς |
| Gen. | ἐμοῦ / μου | ἡμῶν |
| Dat. | ἐμοί / μοι | ἡμῖν |
| Acc. | ἐμέ / με | ἡμᾶς |

Short forms (μου, μοι, με) are enclitic and more common; full forms are emphatic.

### Second Person: σύ / ὑμεῖς

| Case | Sg. | Pl. |
|------|-----|-----|
| Nom. | σύ | ὑμεῖς |
| Gen. | σοῦ / σου | ὑμῶν |
| Dat. | σοί / σοι | ὑμῖν |
| Acc. | σέ / σε | ὑμᾶς |

### Third Person: αὐτός, αὐτή, αὐτό

Declines like a 2-1-2 adjective. Three uses:
1. **Pronoun** (no article nearby): he, she, it, they
2. **Intensive** (predicate position): himself, herself, itself
3. **Identical** (attributive position): the same

## Relative Pronoun: ὅς, ἥ, ὅ (*who, which, that*)

Agrees with antecedent in **gender + number**, but takes its **case from its function in the relative clause**.

| Case | Masc. | Fem. | Neut. |
|------|-------|------|-------|
| Nom. Sg. | ὅς | ἥ | ὅ |
| Gen. Sg. | οὗ | ἧς | οὗ |
| Dat. Sg. | ᾧ | ᾗ | ᾧ |
| Acc. Sg. | ὅν | ἥν | ὅ |
