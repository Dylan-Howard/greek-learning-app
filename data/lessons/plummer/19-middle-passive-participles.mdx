## Middle & Passive Participles — All Tenses

### Present Middle/Passive Participle
Suffix: **-όμενος, -ομένη, -όμενον**
- λύω → λυ**όμενος** (*being loosed / loosing for oneself*)

### Aorist Middle Participle (1st aorist)
Suffix: **-σάμενος, -σαμένη, -σάμενον**
- λύω → λυ**σάμενος** (*having loosed for oneself*)

### Aorist Passive Participle
Suffix: **-θείς, -θεῖσα, -θέν** (3rd & 1st declension)
- λύω → λυ**θείς**, λυ**θεῖσα**, λυ**θέν** (*having been loosed*)

### Perfect Active Participle
Suffix: **-κώς, -κυῖα, -κός** (with reduplication)
- λύω → λελυ**κώς** (*having loosed*)

### Perfect Middle/Passive Participle
Suffix: **-μένος, -μένη, -μένον** (with reduplication)
- λύω → λελυ**μένος** (*having been loosed / in a loosed state*)

## Periphrastic Constructions

εἰμί + participle = compound tense emphasizing continuity:

| εἰμί | Participle | Meaning |
|------|-----------|---------|
| ἦν | pres. ptc. | was ___-ing (continuous past) |
| ἐστίν | perf. ptc. | is in a state of (current result) |
