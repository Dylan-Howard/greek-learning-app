## Contract Verbs

A **contract verb** has a stem ending in **α**, **ε**, or **ο**. When the stem vowel meets the connecting vowel, the two vowels contract into one — and the result takes a **circumflex accent**.

### ε-Contract: ποιέω → *I do, make*

| Person | Sg. | Pl. |
|--------|-----|-----|
| 1st | ποιῶ | ποιοῦμεν |
| 2nd | ποιεῖς | ποιεῖτε |
| 3rd | ποιεῖ | ποιοῦσι(ν) |

Key contractions for ε-stems: ε+ο → ου · ε+ε → ει · ε+ω → ω

### α-Contract: ἀγαπάω → *I love*

| Person | Sg. | Pl. |
|--------|-----|-----|
| 1st | ἀγαπῶ | ἀγαπῶμεν |
| 2nd | ἀγαπᾷς | ἀγαπᾶτε |
| 3rd | ἀγαπᾷ | ἀγαπῶσι(ν) |

Key contractions for α-stems: α+ε → α(long) · α+ο → ω · α+ω → ω

### ο-Contract: πληρόω → *I fill, fulfill*

| Person | Sg. | Pl. |
|--------|-----|-----|
| 1st | πληρῶ | πληροῦμεν |
| 2nd | πληροῖς | πληροῦτε |
| 3rd | πληροῖ | πληροῦσι(ν) |

## Identifying Contract Verbs

In the lexicon, contract verbs show the **full uncontracted form** (ποιέω, not ποιῶ). The circumflex over a contracted syllable is your clue that contraction has occurred.
