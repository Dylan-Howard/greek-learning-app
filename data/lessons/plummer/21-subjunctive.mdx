## The Subjunctive Mood

The subjunctive expresses **possibility, contingency, or intention** — not certainty. It exists in **present** (continuous) and **aorist** (undefined) tenses only.

## How to Identify the Subjunctive

The subjunctive is formed by **lengthening the connecting vowel**:
- ο → ω · ε → η

This lengthening is the primary visual signal.

## Present Active Subjunctive — λύω

| Person | Sg. | Pl. |
|--------|-----|-----|
| 1st | λύ**ω** | λύ**ωμεν** |
| 2nd | λύ**ῃς** | λύ**ητε** |
| 3rd | λύ**ῃ** | λύ**ωσι(ν)** |

## Aorist Active Subjunctive — λύσω (no augment)

| Person | Sg. | Pl. |
|--------|-----|-----|
| 1st | λύ**σω** | λύ**σωμεν** |
| 2nd | λύ**σῃς** | λύ**σητε** |
| 3rd | λύ**σῃ** | λύ**σωσι(ν)** |

## Key Uses of the Subjunctive

| Construction | Signal word | Translation |
|-------------|------------|-------------|
| **Purpose clause** | ἵνα | in order that, so that |
| **3rd class conditional** | ἐάν | if (probable) |
| **Indefinite temporal** | ὅταν | whenever |
| **Prohibition** | μή + aor. subj. | Don't! (command not to start) |
| **Hortatory** | 1st pl. + subj. | Let us ___ |
| **Deliberative** | question form | What should we ___? |
