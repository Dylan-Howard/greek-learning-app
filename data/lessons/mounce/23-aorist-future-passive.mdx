## The Aorist Passive

The **aorist passive** expresses simple, past, passive action: "I was loosed," "he was sent."

It has its own distinctive tense formative: **θη** (or just **η** in some verbs).

Formula: **augment + verb stem + θη + secondary passive endings**

The secondary passive endings are the **secondary active** endings (not middle!):

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | -θην | -θημεν |
| 2nd | -θης | -θητε |
| 3rd | -θη | -θησαν |

**Conjugation of ἐλύθην** (I was loosed):

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | ἐλύ**θην** | ἐλύ**θημεν** |
| 2nd | ἐλύ**θης** | ἐλύ**θητε** |
| 3rd | ἐλύ**θη** | ἐλύ**θησαν** |

## The Future Passive

The future passive = verb stem + θησ + primary middle/passive endings:

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | λυ**θήσομαι** | λυ**θησόμεθα** |
| 2nd | λυ**θήσῃ** | λυ**θήσεσθε** |
| 3rd | λυ**θήσεται** | λυ**θήσονται** |

## Second Aorist Passive

Some verbs form the aorist passive without θ, using just **η** + secondary active endings. These must be memorized as they appear.
