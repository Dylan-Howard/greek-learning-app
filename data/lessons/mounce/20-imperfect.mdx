## The Imperfect Tense

The **imperfect** expresses **continuous action in past time**. It is the past equivalent of the present indicative. The imperfect only occurs in the indicative mood.

The imperfect has two key features:
1. **Augment** — a prefix added to indicate past time
2. **Secondary active endings** — different from present (primary) endings

## The Augment

The augment signals **past time** in the indicative. Two rules:

- **Verb beginning with a consonant**: prefix **ε** → ἔ + λυ = ἔλυ
- **Verb beginning with a vowel**: the initial vowel lengthens:
  - α → η (ἀκούω → ἤκουον)
  - ε → η (ἐγείρω → ἤγειρον)
  - ο → ω (ὁράω → ἑώρων, irregular)
  - αυ → ηυ

## Secondary Active Endings

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | -ον | -ομεν |
| 2nd | -ες | -ετε |
| 3rd | -ε(ν) | -ον |

Notice 1st and 3rd plural are the same (-ον). Context determines person.

## Conjugation: ἔλυον (I was loosing)

| 3rd | ἔλυ**ε(ν)** | ἔλυ**ον** |
