## μι Verbs — The Other Verb Class

Most Greek verbs are **ω verbs** (λύω, γράφω). A smaller but important class are **μι verbs**, which have different personal endings and form some tenses differently. The four most important μι verbs in the NT are:

| Verb | Meaning | Freq. |
|------|---------|-------|
| δίδωμι | I give | 415 |
| τίθημι | I place, put | 100 |
| ἵστημι | I stand, set | 154 |
| ἀφίημι | I forgive, release | 143 |

## Present Active Indicative — δίδωμι (*I give*)

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | δίδω**μι** | δίδο**μεν** |
| 2nd | δίδω**ς** | δίδο**τε** |
| 3rd | δίδω**σι(ν)** | δίδό**ασι(ν)** |

Key difference from ω verbs: no connecting vowel in the singular; the ending attaches directly to the stem.

## Aorist — δίδωμι

The aorist of δίδωμι uses a **κ aorist** (like the perfect): ἔδωκα (*I gave*).

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | ἔδωκα | ἔδομεν (or ἐδώκαμεν) |
| 2nd | ἔδωκας | ἔδοτε |
| 3rd | ἔδωκε(ν) | ἔδοσαν |

## οἶδα — "I Know"

**οἶδα** is a **perfect active** form that has **present meaning** (*I know*, not *I have known*). It is one of the most common verbs in the NT (318 occurrences).

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | οἶδα | οἴδαμεν |
| 2nd | οἶδας | οἴδατε |
| 3rd | οἶδε(ν) | οἴδασι(ν) |

The **pluperfect** of οἶδα serves as its imperfect: ᾔδειν (*I knew*).

## Key μι Verb Forms to Memorize

| Form | Parsing | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| δίδωμι | pres. act. ind. 1sg | I give |
| ἔδωκεν | aor. act. ind. 3sg | he gave |
| δός | aor. act. imp. 2sg | Give! |
| τίθημι | pres. act. ind. 1sg | I place |
| ἔθηκεν | aor. act. ind. 3sg | he placed |
| οἶδα | perf. act. ind. 1sg (pres. meaning) | I know |
| ᾔδειν | pluperf. act. ind. 1sg (past meaning) | I knew |
