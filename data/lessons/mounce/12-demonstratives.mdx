## The Demonstrative Pronouns

Demonstrative pronouns point to something. Greek has two:
- **οὗτος** (*this, these*) — points to something **near** (proximal)
- **ἐκεῖνος** (*that, those*) — points to something **far** (distal)

### The Key Rule: No Article Before the Demonstrative

When a demonstrative modifies a noun, the noun has an article but the demonstrative does **not** take one. The demonstrative stands in **predicate position**.

- **οὗτος** ὁ ἄνθρωπος — *this man* ✓
- ~~ὁ οὗτος ἄνθρωπος~~ — WRONG

### Declining οὗτος (*this*)

| Case | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|
| Nom. Sg. | οὗτος | αὕτη | τοῦτο |
| Gen. Sg. | τούτου | ταύτης | τούτου |
| Dat. Sg. | τούτῳ | ταύτῃ | τούτῳ |
| Acc. Sg. | τοῦτον | ταύτην | τοῦτο |
| Nom. Pl. | οὗτοι | αὗται | ταῦτα |
| Gen. Pl. | τούτων | τούτων | τούτων |
| Dat. Pl. | τούτοις | ταύταις | τούτοις |
| Acc. Pl. | τούτους | ταύτας | ταῦτα |

> **Memory key:** When the stem has ου or αυ, the form begins with a rough breathing (οὗ-, αὕ-). When the stem has τ, the form begins with τ (τοῦ-, τούτ-).

### Declining ἐκεῖνος (*that*)

ἐκεῖνος declines regularly like a 2-1-2 adjective (ἐκεῖνος, ἐκείνη, ἐκεῖνο).

### Substantival Use

Demonstratives frequently stand alone without a noun:
- **τοῦτο** ποιεῖτε — "Do **this**."
- **οὗτοί** εἰσιν — "**These** are..." or "**These men** are..."
