## The Six Components of a Greek Verb

To fully **parse** a Greek verb, you identify six things:

| Component | Options | Example (λύομεν) |
|-----------|---------|-----------------|
| **Tense** | present, future, aorist, imperfect, perfect, pluperfect | present |
| **Voice** | active, middle, passive | active |
| **Mood** | indicative, subjunctive, imperative, optative, infinitive, participle | indicative |
| **Person** | first, second, third | first |
| **Number** | singular, plural | plural |
| **Lexical form + meaning** | the dictionary form | λύω, *I loose* |

So λύομεν = present active indicative, 1st person plural of λύω, "we loose."

## Verbal Aspect — The Key to Greek Tense

Greek tense is primarily about **aspect** — the *kind* of action — not just time.

| Aspect | Kind of Action | Greek Tenses |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| **Continuous** | ongoing, in progress | Present, Imperfect |
| **Undefined** | a simple action, point in time | Aorist, Future |
| **Complete** | completed with ongoing results | Perfect, Pluperfect |

Time is secondary. The **indicative mood** adds a time element (present tense = present time; aorist indicative = past time). But in non-indicative moods (subjunctive, imperative, infinitive, participle), aspect alone is in view — not time.

This is why translating Greek tenses mechanically like English tenses often misleads. Always ask: what kind of action is the author portraying?

## The Structure of a Greek Verb Form

A Greek indicative verb has three parts:

**[Tense Stem] + [Connecting Vowel] + [Personal Ending]**

Example: **λύ** + **ο** + **μεν** = λύομεν

- **Tense stem**: The base form for this tense (λύ- for the present of λύω)
- **Connecting vowel**: Links the stem to the ending (ο before μ and ν; ε elsewhere in present)
- **Personal ending**: Encodes person and number

When you learn a new tense, you're learning: (1) how to form the tense stem and (2) which endings to add.

## Lexical Forms for Verbs

Greek verbs are listed in lexicons in the **first person singular present indicative active**: λύω (*I loose*). This is called the **lexical form**.

From the lexical form, you can always identify the present stem: simply remove the -ω ending.
