## Greek Punctuation

Greek has four punctuation marks. Three overlap with English; one is unique:

| Mark | Looks Like | Meaning |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Period | `.` | End of sentence |
| Comma | `,` | Pause within sentence |
| High dot | `·` | Roughly a semicolon or colon |
| Question mark | `;` (semicolon!) | End of question |

> **Watch out:** The Greek **question mark** looks exactly like an English **semicolon** (`;`). When you see a semicolon at the end of a Greek clause, that's a question mark.

## Syllabification

Every Greek syllable contains **exactly one vowel or diphthong**. To divide a word into syllables:

**Rule 1 — Single consonant:** A single consonant between vowels goes with the following vowel.
- ἀ·πό (*from*) — one consonant π goes with ο

**Rule 2 — Two or more consonants:** When two or more consonants stand between vowels, divide so that the first syllable ends in a consonant if possible. However, if the consonant cluster can begin a Greek word (like πν-, στ-, θρ-), keep them together with the following syllable.
- ἄν·θρω·πος → 3 syllables

**Rule 3 — Compound words:** Compound words divide at the point of composition.
- ἐκ·κλη·σί·α (church) — ἐκ + κλη + σί + α

**Rule 4 — Double consonants:** Double consonants split.
- ἀλ·λά (*but*) — double λ splits

### Vowel length matters

- A syllable with a short vowel is **short**
- A syllable with a long vowel or diphthong is **long**
- The last syllable of a word is the **ultima**
- The second-to-last is the **penult**
- The third-to-last is the **antepenult**

## Accent Marks

Koine Greek manuscripts have three written accent marks (added by later scribes to help readers):

| Accent | Symbol | Name |
|--------|--------|------|
| ´ | acute | Over any syllable |
| ` | grave | Over the ultima only (replaces acute when not at end) |
| ˆ | circumflex | Only over long vowels or diphthongs |

### Basic Rules for Accents

1. The accent can stand on one of the last **three** syllables.
2. If the ultima is **long**, the accent cannot go further back than the **penult**.
3. The **circumflex** can only occur on the **last two** syllables, and only on long syllables.
4. An **acute** on the ultima becomes a **grave** unless the word ends the sentence or clause.

For beginners, the most important thing is to **learn the accent as part of each vocabulary word** rather than deriving it from rules. You will internalize the patterns naturally over time.

> **Practical note:** Most lexicons (including BDAG) list words with their standard accent. When you look a word up, note its accentuation — that helps with parsing later.
