## The Aorist Tense — Overview

The **aorist** expresses **undefined aspect** — a simple, point-in-time action without comment on its progress or duration. In the indicative, it expresses **past time**.

The aorist is the most common past tense in the NT. It is the "default" past tense of narrative.

There are **two types** of aorist: second aorist (covered in this chapter) and first aorist (chapter 22). Both have the same meaning and aspect; they just form differently.

## The Second Aorist

The **second aorist** is formed from a **different stem** than the present — the verbal root with vowel modification. It looks like the imperfect except for the stem change.

Formula: **augment + aorist stem + connecting vowel + secondary active endings**

Key distinction: If you see an augment + secondary endings but the stem is different from the present → **second aorist**.

## Sample Paradigm: βάλλω → second aorist ἔβαλον

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | ἔβαλ**ον** | ἐβάλ**ομεν** |
| 2nd | ἔβαλ**ες** | ἐβάλ**ετε** |
| 3rd | ἔβαλ**ε(ν)** | ἔβαλ**ον** |

Other key second aorist verbs:
| Present | Aorist | Meaning |
|---------|--------|---------|
| ἄγω | ἤγαγον | I led |
| ἔρχομαι | ἦλθον | I came |
| ἐσθίω | ἔφαγον | I ate |
| εὑρίσκω | εὗρον | I found |
| λαμβάνω | ἔλαβον | I took |
| λέγω | εἶπον | I said |
| ὁράω | εἶδον | I saw |
