## εἰμί — Complete Paradigm Review

By now you have encountered εἰμί in multiple tenses. Here is a consolidated reference:

### Present Indicative
| Person | Sg. | Pl. |
|--------|-----|-----|
| 1st | εἰμί | ἐσμέν |
| 2nd | εἶ | ἐστέ |
| 3rd | ἐστίν | εἰσίν |

### Imperfect Indicative
| Person | Sg. | Pl. |
|--------|-----|-----|
| 1st | ἤμην | ἦμεν |
| 2nd | ἦς | ἦτε |
| 3rd | ἦν | ἦσαν |

### Subjunctive (Present)
| Person | Sg. | Pl. |
|--------|-----|-----|
| 1st | ὦ | ὦμεν |
| 2nd | ᾖς | ἦτε |
| 3rd | ᾖ | ὦσι(ν) |

### Imperative (Present)
| Person | Sg. | Pl. |
|--------|-----|-----|
| 2nd | ἴσθι | ἔστε |
| 3rd | ἔστω | ἔστωσαν |

### Infinitive: εἶναι (*to be*)
### Present Participle: ὤν, οὖσα, ὄν (*being*)

## Conditional Sentences

Greek has four types of conditional sentences, distinguished by the conjunction and mood used in the **if-clause** (protasis):

| Class | Type | Protasis | Apodosis |
|-------|------|----------|----------|
| **1st class** | Assumed true | εἰ + indicative | any mood | "If (and it's true)..." |
| **2nd class** | Contrary to fact | εἰ + past indicative | past indic. + ἄν | "If (but it's not)..." |
| **3rd class** | Probable future | ἐάν + subjunctive | any mood | "If (probably)..." |
| **4th class** | Remote possibility | εἰ + optative | optative + ἄν | "If ever (perhaps)..." |

### Examples

**1st class** — real condition: εἰ δὲ Χριστὸς οὐκ ἐγήγερται — "But if Christ has not been raised [and Paul treats it as a real possibility to refute]..." (1 Cor 15:14)

**2nd class** — contrary to fact: εἰ ἐκ τοῦ κόσμου ἦτε, ὁ κόσμος ἂν τὸ ἴδιον ἐφίλει — "If you were of the world [but you're not], the world would love its own." (John 15:19)

**3rd class** — probable future: ἐὰν ὁμολογῶμεν τὰς ἁμαρτίας ἡμῶν — "If we confess our sins [and it's likely we will/should]..." (1 John 1:9)
