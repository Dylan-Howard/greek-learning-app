## What Is a Contract Verb?

A **contract verb** has a stem ending in a vowel — specifically **α**, **ε**, or **ο**. When this stem vowel meets the connecting vowel (also a vowel), the two vowels **contract** (merge) into a single, longer vowel or diphthong. The resulting form has a **circumflex accent** over the contracted syllable.

In the lexicon, contract verbs are listed showing the uncontracted stem: ἀγαπάω, ποιέω, πληρόω.

## Contraction Rules

### ε-contract verbs (e.g., ποιέω → *I do, make*)

| Combination | Result |
|-------------|--------|
| ε + ο | ου |
| ε + ε | ει |
| ε + ω | ω |
| ε + ει | ει |
| ε + ουσι | ουσι |

**ποιέω** conjugated (present active indicative):

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | ποιῶ | ποιοῦμεν |
| 2nd | ποιεῖς | ποιεῖτε |
| 3rd | ποιεῖ | ποιοῦσι(ν) |

### α-contract verbs (e.g., ἀγαπάω → *I love*)

| Combination | Result |
|-------------|--------|
| α + ε | α (long) |
| α + ο | ω |
| α + ει | ᾳ |
| α + ω | ω |
| α + ουσι | ωσι |

**ἀγαπάω** conjugated:

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | ἀγαπῶ | ἀγαπῶμεν |
| 2nd | ἀγαπᾷς | ἀγαπᾶτε |
| 3rd | ἀγαπᾷ | ἀγαπῶσι(ν) |

### ο-contract verbs (e.g., πληρόω → *I fill, fulfill*)

| Combination | Result |
|-------------|--------|
| ο + ε | ου |
| ο + ο | ου |
| ο + ω | ω |
| ο + ει | οι |
| ο + ουσι | ουσι |

**πληρόω** conjugated:

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | πληρῶ | πληροῦμεν |
| 2nd | πληροῖς | πληροῦτε |
| 3rd | πληροῖ | πληροῦσι(ν) |

## Recognizing Contracted Forms

The key identifier is the **circumflex accent** over a long vowel or diphthong in the personal ending. When you see ποιεῖ, you know this is a contracted form: ε + ε → ει.
