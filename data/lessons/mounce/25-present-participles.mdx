## What Is a Participle?

A **participle** is a verbal adjective. It carries properties of both a verb (tense, voice, aspect) and an adjective (gender, number, case, concord with a noun). It is one of the most common constructions in the Greek NT — mastering it unlocks enormous portions of the text.

An **adverbial participle** modifies the verb of the main clause, describing the circumstances of the action: time, cause, manner, condition, concession, means, or purpose.

## Forming the Present Active Participle

The present active participle is formed with the suffix **-ων, -ουσα, -ον** (nominative singular forms):

- Masculine/Neuter: third declension (like ἄρχων)
- Feminine: first declension (like φωνή)

**λύω → λύων, λύουσα, λῦον** (*loosing, while loosing*)

### Present Active Participle Declension (nominative sg. only shown here)

| | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter |
|-|-----------|----------|--------|
| Nom. Sg. | λύ**ων** | λύ**ουσα** | λῦ**ον** |
| Gen. Sg. | λύ**οντος** | λύ**ούσης** | λύ**οντος** |

The full declension follows the third declension (masc./neut.) and first declension (fem.) patterns.

## Forming the Present Middle/Passive Participle

Suffix: **-όμενος, -ομένη, -όμενον** — declines like a 2-1-2 adjective.

**λύομαι → λυόμενος, λυομένη, λυόμενον** (*being loosed*)

## Translating Adverbial Participles

The adverbial participle relates to the **main verb's subject**. It must agree in gender, number, and case with the subject (nominative). Common translations:

- **Temporal**: "While loosing...," "After loosing...," "When he loosed..."
- **Causal**: "Because he was loosing..."
- **Means**: "By loosing..."

The tense of the participle shows **relative time** to the main verb:
- **Present participle** = action *simultaneous* with the main verb
- **Aorist participle** = action *prior* to the main verb
