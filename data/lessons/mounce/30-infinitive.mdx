## What Is an Infinitive?

The **infinitive** is a verbal noun — it names an action ("to loose," "to believe"). It has tense (aspect) and voice but no person, number, or mood. It is **indeclinable**.

The infinitive is used:
1. As the subject or object of a verb
2. In infinitival clauses expressing purpose, time, or result
3. As the complement of certain verbs (δύναμαι, θέλω, μέλλω)
4. In indirect discourse after certain verbs

## Forming Infinitives

| Tense/Voice | Ending | Example |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| Present active | -ειν | λύ**ειν** (*to be loosing*) |
| Present mid/pass | -εσθαι | λύ**εσθαι** (*to be being loosed*) |
| Aorist active (1st) | -σαι | λύ**σαι** (*to loose*) |
| Aorist passive | -θῆναι | λυ**θῆναι** (*to be loosed*) |
| Perfect active | -κέναι | λελυ**κέναι** (*to have loosed*) |

> No augment on the aorist infinitive.

## The Articular Infinitive

When the infinitive takes the **neuter article** (τό, τοῦ, τῷ), it is an **articular infinitive**. The case of the article signals the infinitive's function in the sentence:

- **τοῦ** + infinitive (genitive) = purpose or result: "in order to"
- **τό** + infinitive (accusative) = subject or direct object
- **πρὸ τοῦ** + infinitive = "before ___-ing"
- **ἐν τῷ** + infinitive = "while ___-ing"
- **μετὰ τό** + infinitive = "after ___-ing"

## Purpose Infinitives

Infinitives very commonly express **purpose**:
- ἦλθεν **λῦσαι** τὸν νόμον — "He came **to loose** (i.e., in order to abolish) the law."
