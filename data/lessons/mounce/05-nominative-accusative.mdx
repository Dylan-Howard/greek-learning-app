## The Case System

Greek nouns **change their endings** to show their grammatical function. These changed forms are called **cases**. In this chapter you learn two:

- **Nominative** — the subject of the verb (*The word* is true.)
- **Accusative** — the direct object (*I see the word.*)

The ending of a noun tells you its case; the case tells you its role in the sentence.

## The Article: Most Common Word in the NT

The Greek definite article (ὁ, ἡ, τό) means "the." It appears over 19,000 times in the NT — more than any other word. It **agrees** with the noun it modifies in **gender, number, and case**.

There is no indefinite article in Greek. λόγος can mean "a word" or just "word."

### Definite Article — Nominative & Accusative

|  | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter |
|--|-----------|----------|--------|
| **Nom. Sg.** | ὁ | ἡ | τό |
| **Acc. Sg.** | τόν | τήν | τό |
| **Nom. Pl.** | οἱ | αἱ | τά |
| **Acc. Pl.** | τούς | τάς | τά |

> **Memory tip:** The neuter nominative and accusative are always identical — both singular and plural. Watch for this throughout the paradigms.

## Second Declension Nouns (Masculine)

Second declension nouns are primarily **masculine** (and some neuter). Masculine second declension nouns end in **-ος** in the nominative singular.

**λόγος** (word, reason) — masculine

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nominative | λόγ**ος** | λόγ**οι** |
| Accusative | λόγ**ον** | λόγ**ους** |

The stem is **λογ-**. Add the endings to the stem.

**ἄνθρωπος** (man, human being) — masculine

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nominative | ἄνθρωπ**ος** | ἄνθρωπ**οι** |
| Accusative | ἄνθρωπ**ον** | ἄνθρωπ**ους** |

## First Declension Nouns (Feminine)

First declension nouns are primarily **feminine**. They end in **-η** or **-α** in the nominative singular.

**φωνή** (voice, sound) — feminine, -η stem

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nominative | φων**ή** | φων**αί** |
| Accusative | φων**ήν** | φων**άς** |

**ἀρχή** (beginning, ruler) — feminine

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nominative | ἀρχ**ή** | ἀρχ**αί** |
| Accusative | ἀρχ**ήν** | ἀρχ**άς** |

## Second Declension Neuter Nouns

Neuter nouns follow the second declension but have different endings. Note that neuter **nominative = accusative** always.

**εὐαγγέλιον** (gospel, good news) — neuter

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nominative | εὐαγγέλι**ον** | εὐαγγέλι**α** |
| Accusative | εὐαγγέλι**ον** | εὐαγγέλι**α** |

## Parsing

To **parse** a noun, identify: (1) case, (2) number, (3) gender, (4) lexical form, (5) meaning.

**τόν λόγον** — accusative, singular, masculine, λόγος, "the word" (direct object)

**αἱ ἀρχαί** — nominative, plural, feminine, ἀρχή, "the beginnings/rulers" (subject)
