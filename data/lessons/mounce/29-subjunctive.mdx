## The Subjunctive Mood

The **subjunctive** expresses **possibility, contingency, or intention** — not certainty. It is the "maybe" mood, used in purpose clauses, conditional sentences, deliberative questions, and exhortations.

The subjunctive has only **present** (continuous aspect) and **aorist** (undefined aspect) tenses in the NT. It does not indicate time.

## Forming the Subjunctive

The subjunctive is formed by **lengthening the connecting vowel**:
- ο/ε → **ω/η**

This lengthened vowel is the primary signal that a form is subjunctive.

**Present active subjunctive of λύω:**

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | λύ**ω** | λύ**ωμεν** |
| 2nd | λύ**ῃς** | λύ**ητε** |
| 3rd | λύ**ῃ** | λύ**ωσι(ν)** |

> Note: 1st singular present active indicative (λύω) and 1st singular present active subjunctive (λύω) look identical. Context determines which it is.

**Aorist active subjunctive of λύω** (no augment!):

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | λύ**σω** | λύ**σωμεν** |
| 2nd | λύ**σῃς** | λύ**σητε** |
| 3rd | λύ**σῃ** | λύ**σωσι(ν)** |

## Common Uses of the Subjunctive

**1. Purpose clauses** with ἵνα (*in order that*) — most common use:
- ἦλθεν **ἵνα σώσῃ** τὸν κόσμον — "He came **in order that he might save** the world."

**2. Prohibitions** (μή + aorist subjunctive):
- **μὴ κρίνητε** — "Do not judge!" (stronger than present imperative prohibition)

**3. Hortatory subjunctive** (1st person plural, *let us*):
- **ἀγαπῶμεν** ἀλλήλους — "**Let us love** one another."

**4. Deliberative questions** (what should we do?):
- τί **ποιῶμεν**; — "What **should we do**?"

**5. Third-class conditional** (ἐάν + subjunctive):
- **ἐὰν πιστεύσῃς** — "**If you should believe**..." (probable condition)

## ἵνα — the Subjunctive Signal Word

The conjunction **ἵνα** (*in order that, that, so that*) almost always introduces a subjunctive. When you see ἵνα, expect a subjunctive verb immediately.
