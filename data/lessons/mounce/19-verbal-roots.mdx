## Verbal Roots vs. Present Stems

In many Greek verbs, the **present stem** is not the same as the **verbal root**. The present stem may have been modified (with added letters or vowel changes) to create the present tense form. The future and aorist are built on the **verbal root**, not the present stem.

This is why some verbs look very different from their present form in other tenses:
- βάλλω (present) → βαλ- (verbal root) → βαλῶ (future), ἔβαλον (aorist)
- γινώσκω (present) → γνω- (verbal root) → γνώσομαι (future), ἔγνων (aorist)

## The Six Principal Parts

Greek grammars often present **six principal parts** of a verb — one for each tense stem. You cannot reliably predict all six from just the first. You must learn irregular principal parts as vocabulary.

| # | Tense | Example (λύω) |
|---|-------|--------------|
| 1 | Present active | λύω |
| 2 | Future active | λύσω |
| 3 | Aorist active | ἔλυσα |
| 4 | Perfect active | λέλυκα |
| 5 | Perfect middle/passive | λέλυμαι |
| 6 | Aorist passive | ἐλύθην |

A dash (—) in a principal part means that form does not exist or is not used in the NT.
