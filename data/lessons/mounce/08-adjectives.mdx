## Adjective Agreement

A Greek adjective **must agree** with the noun it modifies in three things:
1. **Gender** (masculine, feminine, neuter)
2. **Number** (singular, plural)
3. **Case** (nominative, genitive, dative, accusative)

The adjective follows the same declension patterns as nouns. A 2-1-2 adjective (the most common type) uses second declension for masculine and neuter, first declension for feminine.

### ἀγαθός (*good*) — 2-1-2 Adjective, Nominative Singular

| Gender | Nominative Sg. |
|--------|---------------|
| Masculine | ἀγαθ**ός** |
| Feminine | ἀγαθ**ή** |
| Neuter | ἀγαθ**όν** |

The adjective declines exactly like the article + noun system you already know. To form any other case, apply the same endings.

## Attributive vs. Predicate Position

This is the most important concept in this chapter.

### Attributive Position — adjective **inside** the article-noun unit

The adjective sits **between the article and the noun**, or follows the article after the noun. It directly modifies the noun.

- **ὁ ἀγαθὸς λόγος** — *the good word* (article–adjective–noun)
- **ὁ λόγος ὁ ἀγαθός** — *the good word* (article–noun–article–adjective)

Both patterns mean the same thing. The adjective is "inside" the article's embrace.

### Predicate Position — adjective **outside** the article-noun unit

The adjective has **no article** before it (or stands completely separate). It makes a **statement** about the noun, like a predicate adjective.

- **ἀγαθὸς ὁ λόγος** — *The word is good.* (adjective–article–noun)
- **ὁ λόγος ἀγαθός** — *The word is good.* (article–noun–adjective, no second article)

> Key test: If the adjective has its own article that agrees with the noun, it's **attributive**. If it lacks the article before it, it's **predicate**.

## Substantival Adjectives

In Greek, an adjective can function as a **noun** (a substantive) when no noun is present. The article signals this.

- **οἱ ἀγαθοί** — *the good men* (or simply "the good ones") — masculine plural with article
- **τὸ ἀγαθόν** — *the good thing* or *goodness* — neuter singular
- **τὰ ἁγία** — *the holy things* — used for the temple sanctuary

Context and the gender/number of the article will guide your translation.
