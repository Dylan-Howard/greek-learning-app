## The First Aorist

The **first (sigmatic) aorist** adds the **tense formative σα** between the verbal root and the secondary endings. This is the most common aorist type.

Formula: **augment + verb stem + σα + secondary endings**

Note: The σ of the tense formative causes the same consonant changes as the future (labials → ψ, velars → ξ, dentals drop).

## First Aorist Active Endings

The σα absorbs into the personal ending: the 3rd singular is **σε(ν)**, not σα + ε.

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | -σα | -σαμεν |
| 2nd | -σας | -σατε |
| 3rd | -σε(ν) | -σαν |

## Conjugation: ἔλυσα (I loosed)

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | ἔλυ**σα** | ἐλύ**σαμεν** |
| 2nd | ἔλυ**σας** | ἐλύ**σατε** |
| 3rd | ἔλυ**σε(ν)** | ἔλυ**σαν** |

## First Aorist Middle Endings: -σάμην, -σω, -σατο...

The first aorist middle substitutes the secondary middle endings after the σα formative.

## First vs. Second Aorist — How to Tell Them Apart

- **First aorist**: augmented present stem + σα + secondary endings (ἔλυσα)
- **Second aorist**: augmented different (root) stem + secondary endings, no σ (ἔβαλον)

If you see σα before secondary endings → first aorist.
