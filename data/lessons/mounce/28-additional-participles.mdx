## Periphrastic Constructions

A **periphrastic** construction uses a form of εἰμί + a participle to form a compound verb phrase. This is especially common in Mark and Luke.

- ἦν **διδάσκων** — "he was teaching" (imperfect εἰμί + present participle)
- ἔσται **γεγραμμένος** — "it will have been written" (future εἰμί + perfect participle)

Common periphrastic combinations:

| εἰμί tense | Participle tense | Resulting meaning |
|-----------|-----------------|------------------|
| Present | Present | Continuous present action |
| Imperfect | Present | Continuous past action |
| Future | Present | Continuous future action |
| Present | Perfect | Current resulting state |

## Indirect Discourse with Participles

After verbs of perception (seeing, hearing, knowing), Greek sometimes uses a **participle** in the accusative to express what was perceived:

- εἶδον αὐτὸν **ἐρχόμενον** — "I saw him *coming*" (I perceived him in the act)
- ἤκουσαν αὐτοῦ **λέγοντος** — "they heard him *speaking*"

The participle agrees with the person perceived (accusative or genitive depending on the verb).

## Summary of Participle Uses

| Use | Article | Position | Translation |
|-----|---------|----------|-------------|
| Adverbial | None | Any case | while, after, because, by |
| Adjectival | Yes | Attributive | the _-ing noun |
| Substantive | Yes | No noun | the one who ___ |
| Periphrastic | N/A | With εἰμί | was ___-ing |
| Indirect discourse | None | Accusative | saw/heard him ___-ing |
