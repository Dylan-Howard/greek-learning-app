## The Perfect Tense

The **perfect** expresses a **completed action with ongoing results**. The emphasis is on the present state or result that a past action has produced.

- λέλυκα — "I have loosed" (and it remains loosed)
- γέγραπται — "it is written" (and stands written)

The perfect is extremely theologically rich in the NT. John 19:30 τετέλεσται (*It is finished*) is perfect tense — "it has been completed and stands complete."

## How the Perfect Is Formed

The perfect uses **reduplication** — the first consonant of the verb is repeated with an ε inserted, forming a prefix that marks this tense.

**Reduplication rule:**
- Verb starting with a consonant: repeat the consonant + ε → λύω → **λε**λυκα
- Verb starting with a vowel: the vowel lengthens (like augment) → ἀγαπάω → **ἠγ**άπηκα

## Perfect Active Endings (κ tense formative)

The tense formative is **κ** (perfect active only):

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | -κα | -καμεν |
| 2nd | -κας | -κατε |
| 3rd | -κε(ν) | -κασι(ν) |

**Conjugation: λέλυκα** (I have loosed)

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | λέλυ**κα** | λελύ**καμεν** |
| 2nd | λέλυ**κας** | λελύ**κατε** |
| 3rd | λέλυ**κε(ν)** | λελύ**κασι(ν)** |

## Perfect Middle/Passive (no κ formative)

The perfect middle/passive is formed with reduplication + primary middle/passive endings (no κ):

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | λέλυ**μαι** | λελύ**μεθα** |
| 2nd | λέλυ**σαι** | λέλυ**σθε** |
| 3rd | λέλυ**ται** | λέλυ**νται** |

## Theologically Important Perfects in the NT

- **γέγραπται** — "it is written" (γράφω) — used when citing Scripture
- **τετέλεσται** — "it is finished/completed" (τελέω) — John 19:30
- **πεπίστευκεν** — "he has believed (and remains a believer)" (πιστεύω)
- **σέσωκεν** — "he has saved (and that salvation stands)" (σῴζω)
