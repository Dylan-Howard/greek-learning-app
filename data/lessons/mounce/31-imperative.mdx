## The Imperative Mood

The **imperative** mood expresses a **command** or **prohibition**. Greek imperatives come in present (continuous) and aorist (undefined) aspect, and in second and third person.

- **Present imperative**: command for ongoing/repeated action ("Keep praying!")
- **Aorist imperative**: command for a simple act ("Pray!" — do it)

The distinction is significant: the **aspect** of the command communicates how the author envisions the action.

## Present Active Imperative Endings

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 2nd | -ε | -ετε |
| 3rd | -έτω | -έτωσαν |

**λύω → present active imperative:**

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 2nd | λῦ**ε** (*Loose!*) | λύ**ετε** (*Loose! [pl.]*) |
| 3rd | λυ**έτω** (*Let him loose!*) | λυ**έτωσαν** (*Let them loose!*) |

## Aorist Active Imperative Endings

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 2nd | -σον | -σατε |
| 3rd | -σάτω | -σάτωσαν |

**λύω → aorist active imperative:**

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 2nd | λῦ**σον** (*Loose!*) | λύ**σατε** |
| 3rd | λυ**σάτω** | λυ**σάτωσαν** |

## Prohibition Forms

- **Present imperative + μή** = stop doing (ongoing action): μὴ κλαίετε — "Stop weeping."
- **Aorist subjunctive + μή** = don't do (undefined): μὴ νομίσητε — "Don't think that..."

## Middle/Passive Imperatives

Middle/passive imperatives use: -ου (2nd sg), -έσθω (3rd sg), -εσθε (2nd pl), -έσθωσαν (3rd pl).
