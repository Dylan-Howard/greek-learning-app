## The Present Active Indicative

The **present tense** in Greek expresses **continuous aspect** — action that is ongoing or repeated. In the **indicative mood**, it refers to present time. The **active voice** means the subject is performing the action.

A present active indicative verb communicates: *right now, the subject is in the process of doing this.*

## The Endings

The present active indicative uses the **primary active personal endings**. These same endings appear (with slight modifications) in the future and perfect tenses as well.

**Full conjugation of λύω (*I loose*)**

| Person | Singular | Translation | Plural | Translation |
|--------|----------|-------------|--------|-------------|
| 1st | λύ**ω** | I loose | λύ**ομεν** | we loose |
| 2nd | λύ**εις** | you loose | λύ**ετε** | you (all) loose |
| 3rd | λύ**ει** | he/she/it looses | λύ**ουσι(ν)** | they loose |

## The Connecting Vowel Rule

Between the stem and the personal ending sits a **connecting vowel** (also called a thematic vowel):
- **ο** before **μ** and **ν** (first and third plural)
- **ε** everywhere else

The 1st singular **-ω** = the connecting vowel ο + the 1st sg. ending (nothing visible, the ο lengthens to ω). The 3rd singular **-ει** = ε + ι (contract).

You don't need to memorize this mechanically — just learn the full endings above. The rule explains *why* they look the way they do.

## The Movable Nu

The 3rd plural ending **-ουσι** often adds a final **ν** to become **-ουσιν** when followed by a vowel or at the end of a sentence. This is called the **movable nu** (ν-movable). You will see both forms: λύουσι and λύουσιν.

## Parsing Practice

To parse a present active indicative form:
1. Remove the ending to find the stem
2. Identify person and number from the ending
3. State: present / active / indicative / [person] / [number] / [lexical form] / [meaning]

**Examples:**
- πιστεύετε → present active indicative, 2nd plural, πιστεύω, "you believe"
- ἀκούομεν → present active indicative, 1st plural, ἀκούω, "we hear"
- γράφει → present active indicative, 3rd singular, γράφω, "he/she writes"

## Translation

The present active indicative can be translated several ways depending on context:
- **Simple**: "he looses"
- **Progressive**: "he is loosing"
- **Customary**: "he loosens (regularly)"

Greek doesn't always distinguish these — context decides.
