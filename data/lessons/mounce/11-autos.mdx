## αὐτός — Three Distinct Uses

αὐτός is the most common word in the entire Greek New Testament (5,597 occurrences). It has **three distinct uses** determined entirely by its position relative to the article.

### 1. Personal Pronoun (no article nearby)
αὐτός functions as a third-person pronoun: he, she, it, they.
- εἶδεν **αὐτόν** — "he saw **him**"
- ἔλεγεν **αὐτοῖς** — "he was saying to **them**"

### 2. Intensive Pronoun (predicate position)
αὐτός in predicate position (no article before it) intensifies a noun: *himself, herself, itself*.
- **αὐτὸς** ὁ κύριος — "the Lord **himself**"
- εἶπεν **αὐτὸς** ὁ Ἰησοῦς — "Jesus **himself** said"

### 3. Identical Adjective (attributive position)
αὐτός in attributive position (article + αὐτός + noun or article + noun + article + αὐτός) means *same*.
- ὁ **αὐτὸς** λόγος — "the **same** word"
- τὴν **αὐτὴν** πίστιν — "the **same** faith"

## Declining αὐτός

αὐτός declines exactly like a 2-1-2 adjective (like ἀγαθός). The forms are identical to what you learned in chapter 8.

| Case | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|
| Nom. Sg. | αὐτός | αὐτή | αὐτό |
| Gen. Sg. | αὐτοῦ | αὐτῆς | αὐτοῦ |
| Dat. Sg. | αὐτῷ | αὐτῇ | αὐτῷ |
| Acc. Sg. | αὐτόν | αὐτήν | αὐτό |
| Nom. Pl. | αὐτοί | αὐταί | αὐτά |
| Gen. Pl. | αὐτῶν | αὐτῶν | αὐτῶν |
| Dat. Pl. | αὐτοῖς | αὐταῖς | αὐτοῖς |
| Acc. Pl. | αὐτούς | αὐτάς | αὐτά |
