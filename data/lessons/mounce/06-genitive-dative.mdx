## The Genitive Case

The **genitive** most commonly expresses **possession** or a close relationship between two nouns. It typically translates as "of ___" or with an English possessive ('s).

- ὁ λόγος τοῦ θεοῦ — "the word **of God**" (the word that belongs to or comes from God)
- ἡ ἀγάπη τοῦ κυρίου — "the love **of the Lord**"

The genitive also expresses: description (*man of faith*), source (*from God*), separation (*apart from sin*), and several other relationships you will encounter in exegesis.

## The Dative Case

The **dative** most commonly functions as the **indirect object** — the person to whom or for whom an action is done. It translates as "to ___," "for ___," or sometimes "with ___."

- λέγω τῷ ἀνθρώπῳ — "I speak **to the man**"
- πιστεύω τῷ κυρίῳ — "I believe **in the Lord**"

The dative is also used with certain prepositions and to express means, manner, and location.

## Full Noun Paradigm — First & Second Declension

### Second Declension Masculine: λόγος (word)

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nominative | λόγ**ος** | λόγ**οι** |
| Genitive | λόγ**ου** | λόγ**ων** |
| Dative | λόγ**ῳ** | λόγ**οις** |
| Accusative | λόγ**ον** | λόγ**ους** |

> The **dative singular** has an iota subscript (ῳ). The **genitive plural** ends in -ων (common to all declensions).

### First Declension Feminine: φωνή (voice)

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nominative | φων**ή** | φων**αί** |
| Genitive | φων**ῆς** | φων**ῶν** |
| Dative | φων**ῇ** | φων**αῖς** |
| Accusative | φων**ήν** | φων**άς** |

### Second Declension Neuter: εὐαγγέλιον (gospel)

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| Nominative | εὐαγγέλι**ον** | εὐαγγέλι**α** |
| Genitive | εὐαγγελί**ου** | εὐαγγελί**ων** |
| Dative | εὐαγγελί**ῳ** | εὐαγγελί**οις** |
| Accusative | εὐαγγέλι**ον** | εὐαγγέλι**α** |

## The Full Article Paradigm

| Case | Masc. | Fem. | Neut. |
|------|-------|------|-------|
| Nom. Sg. | ὁ | ἡ | τό |
| Gen. Sg. | τοῦ | τῆς | τοῦ |
| Dat. Sg. | τῷ | τῇ | τῷ |
| Acc. Sg. | τόν | τήν | τό |
| Nom. Pl. | οἱ | αἱ | τά |
| Gen. Pl. | τῶν | τῶν | τῶν |
| Dat. Pl. | τοῖς | ταῖς | τοῖς |
| Acc. Pl. | τούς | τάς | τά |

> The genitive plural **τῶν** is the same across all three genders. The article is your best friend for identifying case when a noun's ending is ambiguous.

## The Master Case Endings

A key insight in Mounce's approach: there is a set of **master case endings** that recur across all paradigms. Learning them now pays dividends throughout the course:

| Case | Masc./Neut. Sg. | Fem. Sg. | Masc./Neut. Pl. | Fem. Pl. |
|------|-----------------|----------|-----------------|---------|
| Gen. | ου / ου | ης / ης | ων | ων |
| Dat. | ῳ (+ ι-sub) | ῃ (+ ι-sub) | οις | αις |
