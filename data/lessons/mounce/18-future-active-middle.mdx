## How the Future Is Formed

The future tense is formed by adding the **tense formative σ** directly to the verbal root, then attaching the primary active or middle/passive endings with the connecting vowel.

**Formula**: Verbal root + **σ** + connecting vowel + personal ending

**λύω** → stem **λυ** + **σ** = **λυσ-** → λύσω, λύσεις, λύσει...

### Future Active Indicative of λύω

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | λύ**σω** | λύ**σομεν** |
| 2nd | λύ**σεις** | λύ**σετε** |
| 3rd | λύ**σει** | λύ**σουσι(ν)** |

The forms are identical to the present active, except the stem has **σ** inserted.

### Future Middle Indicative (Deponent example: ἔρχομαι)

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | ἐλεύ**σομαι** | ἐλευ**σόμεθα** |
| 2nd | ἐλεύ**σῃ** | ἐλεύ**σεσθε** |
| 3rd | ἐλεύ**σεται** | ἐλεύ**σονται** |

Note: ἔρχομαι has a different future stem (ἐλευ-). Irregular futures must be memorized.

## Consonant Changes Before σ

When the verb stem ends in a consonant, adding σ causes **predictable changes**:

| Stem-final consonant | + σ → |
|---------------------|-------|
| π, β, φ (labials) | ψ |
| κ, γ, χ (velars) | ξ |
| τ, δ, θ (dentals) | σ (dental drops) |

Example: βλέπ**ω** → βλέπ + σ → βλέ**ψ**ω

## Liquid Futures

Verbs with stems ending in **λ, μ, ν, ρ** (liquids and nasals) do not add σ directly. Instead they:
1. Use ε as a tense formative (not σ)
2. Contract the ε with the connecting vowel
3. The result looks like a present contract verb but is future

Example: **κρίνω** (*I judge*) → future: κρινῶ, κρινεῖς, κρινεῖ...
